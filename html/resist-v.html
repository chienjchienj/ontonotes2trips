resist

1.1: fight back or fend off attack by someone or something

  • The soldiers resisted (the enemy attacks) for two days.
  • We were the village that had resisted the invaders!

1.2: remain firm against the actions and effects

  • The party leader resisted demands for his resignation.
  • He tried to run away from the police and was charged with resisting arrest.
  • The new hybrid crops are much better at resisting disease.
  • No one could resist her charms.
  • Majestic buildings with limestone vaults which have resisted the test of time.
  • My son resists any school assignment, regardless of how easy it is.

1.3: elude especially in a baffling way

  • They point to a world that actively resists logic and plain sense.
  • Haiku resists explanation, but you know it when you see it.
