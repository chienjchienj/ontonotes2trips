ask

1.1: inquire, seek an answer

  • You asked that question before.
  • They were asking about John's mother's health.
  • I asked directions to the art museum.
  • Let's ask Professor Smith for an explanation of Calabi-Yau space.
  • Let's ask the Tarot cards about our future.

1.2: request or demand something from someone

  • I think he'll ask her for a divorce.
  • She asked her boss for a new computer.
  • He asked only to be left in peace.
  • This is asking a lot of such young children.
  • They ask that their employees only smoke in designated areas.

1.3: specify a price or value for something

  • John thought the gallery asked too much for that painting.
  • The kidnappers were asking a million dollars for their hostage.
  • They usually ask a higher price than what they really expect to get.

1.4: need, call for

  • This dish asks for a little pepper to spice it up.
  • This run-down old house is just asking for some renovation.
  • His job asks for a lot of patience and focus.

1.5: acting in a manner that results in specific consequences

  • He's asking for trouble.
  • He's asking for a beating.
  • More than two pieces of luggage and you're asking for a world of stress.

1.6: invite

  • Should a woman ask a man out for a date?
  • I asked her in for a drink, but she politely refused.
  • Why don't we ask the neighbors over for a barbeque?
  • I'm trying to pluck up the courage to ask him round for a drink.
