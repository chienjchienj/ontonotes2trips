hear

1.1: learn, receive information, take heed of

  • I hear she just got married.
  • We heard rumors to that effect.
  • She's heard nothing about or from him in years.
  • The committee would like to hear the opinions of the two Nobel laureates on this matter.
  • She just doesn't want to hear her mother's advice.
  • Hear me out!

1.2: perceive physical sound

  • My father doesn't hear as well as he used to.
  • The dog heard footsteps on the front porch.

1.3: examine evidence in a legal setting

  • The jury had heard all the evidence.
  • The judge will hear your case next week.
