enforce

1.1: compel to obey a rule or behave in certain way

  • How is child support enforced?
  • Perhaps it is time to enforce the law.
  • We will enforce the wearing of helmets.
  • We would need too many rules to enforce courtesy.

1.2: strengthen; give force to

  • The Inc. 5000 recognition enforces our belief that you can enjoy what
  • you do while exceeding customer expectations.
  • Besides carefully revising the chapters, I have here and there
  • enforced the argument by a further illustration.
