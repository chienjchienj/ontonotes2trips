own

1.1: have, possess or control something

  • Bob owns a house on the Cape.
  • The French conglomerate owns a controlling share of that manufacturing firm.
  • The critics say that this young soprano could own Verdi in a few more years.
  • The hermit owns nothing but the clothes on his back.
  • As manager Mary now owns all the problems of her predecessor.

1.2: admit or acknowledge

  • He owned to having stolen the teacher's watch.
  • They won't own up to having participated in insider trading.
  • The commissioner finally owned that there would be fees associated with using the new park.
