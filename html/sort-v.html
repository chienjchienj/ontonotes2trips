sort

1.1: Examine to test suitability.

  • Scientists sorted the samples and discarded the ones collected before 1987.
  • The company sorted the applicants into a pile to reject and a pile to interview.

1.2: Arrange by class or category.

  • How would you sort these pottery shards - are they prehistoric?
  • Linguists often try to sort verbs into classes.

1.3: Make clear, free from ambiguity.

  • Let us sort out the question of who is at fault here.
  • I'm glad we got that sorted out after being so confused.

1.4: Punish in order to gain control or enforce obedience.

  • The teacher sorted out the students rather frequently.
